# Oracle Health (Cerner) AI Research Report

**Research Date:** November 21, 2025
**Vendor:** Oracle Health (formerly Cerner Corporation)
**Acquisition:** Oracle acquired Cerner for $28 billion in June 2022

---

## Executive Summary

Oracle Health is undergoing a major transformation, rebuilding the legacy Cerner EHR platform from the ground up as an "AI-first" system built on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure. The company launched its next-generation ambulatory EHR in August 2025, with acute care functionality planned for 2026. While Oracle Health continues to lose market share to Epic (22.9% vs 42.3% in acute care), early adopters of Oracle's Clinical AI Agent report strong satisfaction with a 30% reduction in documentation time.

## 2. Clinical Documentation AI

### Documentation Workflow

1. Provider activates Clinical AI Agent via mobile app
2. System records provider-patient conversation
3. AI transcribes in multiple languages
4. Generative AI produces structured clinical note draft
5. Provider reviews, edits as needed
6. AI suggests follow-up actions (labs, referrals, etc.)
7. Automated coding suggestions generated

### Performance Metrics (from customer reports)

## 5. Post-Acquisition AI Strategy

### Larry Ellison's Vision

> "Our biggest differentiator is the quality of our AI agents. When a doctor consults with a patient, we listen to that consultation, and we record all the prescriptions, all the doctor's orders, all the doctor's notes... The interface to our system is voice, primarily voice. The whole system is made up of AI agents."

### Strategic Priorities

1. **Complete Platform Rebuild**
   - Using APEX AI Code Generator to rebuild Cerner platform
   - Original build: 25 years
   - AI-assisted rebuild target: 3 years
   - 10x developer productivity improvement claimed

2. **Voice-First Interface**
   - Primary interface is voice, not keyboard/mouse
   - AI agents handle transcription, documentation, suggestions

3. **Healthcare Ecosystem Integration**
   - Goal: Automate and interconnect entire healthcare ecosystem
   - Components: Patients, providers, payers, pharma, banks, government

4. **National Health Database**
   - Building unified national database of medical records
   - Improved information access for healthcare professionals

5. **Security Innovation**
   - Eliminating passwords entirely
   - Biometric authentication only

### AI Technology Approach

- **RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)**: Access to latest medical literature, test results, clinical trials
- **Agentic AI**: Autonomous agents that surface insights and suggest actions
- **Semantic Database**: Built specifically for AI, enabling "live, evergreen data"

## 10. 2025-2026 Roadmap

### 2025 Milestones

| Timeline | Milestone |
|----------|-----------|
| **August 2025** | Next-gen ambulatory EHR launched |
| **Q3-Q4 2025** | Early adopter program expansion |
| **2025** | Complete acute and specialty use cases |
| **2025** | Biometric authentication rollout |
| **Late 2025** | Ambient nursing tools preview |

### 2026 Roadmap

| Timeline | Milestone |
|----------|-----------|
| **2026** | Full acute care functionality |
| **2026** | Functional parity with Millennium |
| **2026** | Seamless upgrade path from legacy |
| **2026** | Patient engagement reimagining |
| **2026** | AI-driven administration/reimbursement |

### Migration Strategy

1. **Dual Posting**: Data flows to both Millennium and new EHR
2. **Automatic Learning**: System pre-populates client content
3. **Sidecar Deployment**: AI features run as overlay on Millennium
4. **Phased Rollout**: Gradual transition by specialty/department
5. **Functional Parity**: Target achievement by 2026

### Future AI Predictions

Industry analysts predict Oracle's AI agents will:
- Automate ~50% of administrative tasks (prior auth, documentation)
- Significantly reduce clinician burnout
- Enable physicians to see more patients daily
